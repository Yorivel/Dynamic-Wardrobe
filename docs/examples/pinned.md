# Pinned Outfits

## Pinning Blocks All Swaps

V's outfits: **`outdoor casual`**, **`street tough`**, **`!tactical stealth`**

| What Happens | Outfit | Why |
|-------------|--------|-----|
| Equip **`!tactical stealth`** via Equipment-EX | **`!tactical stealth`** | Pinned |
| Walk to Kabuki (street area) | **`!tactical stealth`** | Pin blocks location swap |
| Enter a danger zone | **`!tactical stealth`** | Pin blocks combat swap |
| Walk to Corpo Plaza | **`!tactical stealth`** | Pin blocks everything |

## Home Entry Consumes the Pin

V's outfits: **`home cozy`**, **`!tactical stealth`**

| What Happens | Outfit | Why |
|-------------|--------|-----|
| Equip **`!tactical stealth`** | **`!tactical stealth`** | Pinned |
| Walk into the apartment | **`home cozy`** | Home overrides — pin consumed |
| Leave the apartment | Normal swapping resumes | Pin is gone |

To re-pin, just equip the `!` outfit again via Equipment-EX.

## Ripper Temporarily Overrides

V's outfits: **`ripper gown`**, **`!tactical stealth`**

| What Happens | Outfit | Why |
|-------------|--------|-----|
| Equip **`!tactical stealth`** | **`!tactical stealth`** | Pinned |
| Sit down at a ripperdoc | **`ripper gown`** | Scene override |
| Leave the ripperdoc | **`!tactical stealth`** | Pin restored — not consumed |

Unlike home entry, the ripper is a temporary scene override. The pinned outfit comes back when the scene ends.

## Unpinning

Equip any non-`!` outfit via Equipment-EX and the mod takes over again. No special steps needed.

## Matching Exclusion

An outfit named **`!home cozy`** will never be automatically picked as a home outfit. The `!` prefix tells the mod to ignore it entirely during matching — it only activates when you manually equip it.
